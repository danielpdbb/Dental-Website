Predictive Analytics Overview
Dental Website System
PurposeThis document explains how the dental website can use Decision Tree analytics for appointment scheduling and management KPIs, and Regression Analysis for treatment support and next-visit recommendations. These analytics are decision-support tools and do not replace the final judgment of clinic staff or the dentist.
1. Decision Tree Algorithm
Predictive Appointment Scheduling and Management Dashboard Analytics
The Decision Tree Algorithm is used to predict and recommend suitable appointment slots based on patient history, appointment patterns, procedure details, dentist availability, and clinic workload. It can support the patient booking page, the receptionist dashboard, and the management dashboard.
A. Patient Booking Side
When a patient books an appointment through the website, the system can suggest the most suitable available appointment slots. The system may analyze the patient appointment history, preferred schedule, procedure type, estimated procedure duration, dentist availability, clinic peak hours, and previous no-show or cancellation behavior.

Current Treatment Support and Next-Visit Recommendation
Regression Analysis is used to support dental treatment planning. It analyzes patient symptoms, oral health behaviors, clinical indicators, and dentist findings to recommend possible treatments or follow-up procedures. It can be used in two stages: before or during the current appointment using the patient form, and after the procedure using the dentist clinical findings form.
Stage 1: Patient Form for Possible Current Treatment Recommendation
For every appointment, the patient can answer a pre-appointment assessment form. This form collects information about symptoms, pain level, oral health habits, and dental concerns. The answers are used as input for regression analysis.

Dentist Side View
On the dentist dashboard, the dentist can view the appointment details, the patient's pre-appointment form answers, and the regression-based possible current treatment recommendation. This helps the dentist prepare before checking the patient. However, the system only provides decision support. The dentist still performs the actual examination and makes the final treatment decision.
